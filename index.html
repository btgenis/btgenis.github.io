More often than not, the things we see are neither important nor interesting: but the way we see them — that’s a different story. When we come across a self-publication, what excites us are not the images themselves, but how that person sees the world and chooses to frame it. The way even the most ordinary person experiences the world—and, more importantly, the way they choose to share it—will always be intriguing to me. And as the allure of the way we see it exceeds the picture itself, for some of us, the way we present it is far more fascinating than the gallery itself: This website is, essentially, a zine.

THE ZINE

Do you remember the time before the monotonous feeds of social media conquered—nearly—all parts of the web? The internet was something else, wasn't it? For anyone willing to acquire basic knowledge, making websites is still the best way of self-publishing. Because the way we present things is as equally important as the things we present.
